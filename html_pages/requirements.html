Project Background

Avanade is a steering member of the Green Software Foundation, an open-source group that collaborates with several businesses worldwide on green software initiatives. Green software is aware of its resource usage and is flexible in terms of its deployment location and timing. Volvo, a multinational corporation with a strong commitment to sustainability, has a specific focus on reducing carbon emissions from its online sales. A forecasting board that provides guidance on reducing carbon emissions aligns well with Volvo's sustainability goals and the philosophy of the Green Software Foundation. To effectively analyze specific sources and trends, it is recommended to create a visual dashboard that displays historical carbon emissions data.

Project Goals

This project's objective is to create a Carbon Analysis Dashboard for Volvo that offers in-the-moment information and analysis on the carbon emissions connected to the business's operations. To give a precise and thorough overview of Volvo's carbon impact, the dashboard will employ carbon emission data kept in Microsoft Azure cloud service.

In order to lessen its environmental impact, Volvo will be able to measure and analyse its carbon emissions across all of its global operations with the aid of the dashboard and pinpoint areas for improvement. Volvo will be able to determine the most significant sources of emissions and set priorities for its attempts to reduce them thanks to the detailed information we will provide on carbon emissions by source, location, and activity.

The dashboard will also give Volvo information on the carbon emissions linked to its supply chain, allowing the company to collaborate with its suppliers to lower emissions and advance sustainable business practises all along the supply chain. This will be displayed by scope 1,2 and 3 of emissions.

The ultimate goal of this project is to help Volvo achieve its sustainability goals and become a leader in the automotive industry in terms of environmental responsibility. By providing a comprehensive and transparent view of its carbon emissions, Volvo can make data-driven decisions to reduce its environmental impact and promote sustainability throughout its operations and supply chain.

Additionally, using data from current emissions and usage trends, the Carbon Analytic Dashboard will use machine learning to forecast future carbon usage. Volvo will be able to use this to spot possible problem areas and take preventative action to cut emissions before they happen.

To make precise predictions of future carbon usage, a machine learning model will be trained on historical carbon emission data stored in Microsoft Azure. Volvo will be able to make better decisions about its sustainability programmes and significantly enhance its environmental performance by integrating machine intelligence into the dashboard. The Carbon Analysis Dashboard will not only enable proactive management of future emissions but also insights into existing carbon emissions.

Requirement Gathering

After assessing our alternatives for obtaining requirements, we decided to conduct a semi-structured interview because it would enable us to go further and ask additional questions as necessary.

We chose to interview our 2 main end users – the sales infrastructure manager (who oversee planning, installation, maintenance, and upgrade of IT systems) and the marketing manager (who’s role is to present the strengths of Volvo). We picked different questions for both our clients to personalise the requirements gathered to their role.

Personas

After conducting interviews, we made two persona pages respectively for each of the interviewees. This helps us better understand their needs and goals and identify their requirements. Also, we wrote a short story for each of their use cases.
